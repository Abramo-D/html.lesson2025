About the company

Nintendo was not always a video game manufacturer. The origins of the company date all the way back to 1889. For the first 70 years Nintendo was a card production company. First producing hand painted Hanafuda cards. In 1902 Nintendo produced the first western Style playing cards in Japan. In the 1960’s Nintendo ventured into many other types of businesses such as producing toys.

In the 1970’s Nintendo started their first ventures into producing video games, with products such as the TV Game 15, and TV Game 6. In 1980’s upon seeing a board businessman playing with a Calculator, the Game and Watch was bought to life. In 1981 Nintendo expanded to the west, and established Nintendo of America. Leading to the Arcade breakout hit Donkey Kong, selling 170,000 machines. This success paving the way for Nintendo to continue to produce quality video game entertainment.
